## What is Topical Authority?

### Definition

**Topical Authority is the perceived expertise, authoritativeness, and trustworthiness of a website on a specific topic or subject area.**

It's about being recognized as the leading source of information on a particular topic, rather than just optimizing for individual keywords.

### Key Concepts

**1. Topic-First Approach**
- Focus on topics, not just keywords
- Cover subjects comprehensively
- Build depth and breadth
- Establish expertise

**2. Semantic Relevance**
- Use related terms naturally
- Cover topic thoroughly
- Include synonyms and variations
- Build semantic connections

**3. Content Depth**
- Comprehensive coverage
- Detailed information
- Multiple perspectives
- Expert-level detail

**4. Authority Signals**
- Quality backlinks from authoritative sites
- Mentions and citations
- User engagement
- Brand searches
- Social signals

### Topical Authority vs Traditional SEO

**Traditional SEO:**
- Focus on individual keywords
- Optimize one page per keyword
- Build backlinks to individual pages
- Rank for specific search terms

**Topical Authority:**
- Focus on entire topics
- Create topic clusters
- Build authority across topic area
- Rank for entire keyword sets
- Dominate subject areas

## Topic Clusters & Pillar Pages

### What are Topic Clusters?

**Definition:** A content organization model where a comprehensive "pillar" page covers a main topic, and related "cluster" pages cover subtopics, all interlinked to establish topical authority.

### Components

**1. Pillar Page**
- Comprehensive coverage of main topic
- 3,000-5,000+ words
- Links to all cluster pages
- Updated regularly
- Definitive resource

**2. Cluster Pages**
- Focus on specific subtopics
- 1,500-2,500 words
- Link back to pillar page
- Link to other cluster pages
- Detailed coverage

**3. Internal Linking**
- Cluster pages link to pillar
- Pillar links to all clusters
- Clusters interlink where relevant
- Creates content network
- Distributes authority

### Topic Cluster Structure

**Example: SEO Topic Cluster**

**Pillar Page:** "The Complete Guide to SEO"
- Overview of SEO
- Links to all subtopics

**Cluster Pages:**
- "Technical SEO Guide"
- "On-Page SEO Guide"
- "Off-Page SEO Guide"
- "Local SEO Guide"
- "Ecommerce SEO Guide"
- "SEO Tools Guide"

**Sub-Clusters:**
- Under Technical SEO:
  - "Site Speed Optimization"
  - "Mobile SEO"
  - "Schema Markup"
  - "XML Sitemaps"

### Creating Effective Pillar Pages

**Content Requirements:**
- Comprehensive coverage

## Internal Linking for Topical Authority

### Importance of Internal Linking

**Benefits:**
- Distributes link equity
- Helps crawlers discover content
- Establishes topical relevance
- Improves user navigation
- Increases time on site
- Reduces bounce rate

### Internal Linking Strategy

**1. Pillar to Cluster Linking**
- Pillar page links to all cluster pages
- Use descriptive anchor text
- Link naturally within content
- Link in relevant sections
- Link in table of contents

**2. Cluster to Pillar Linking**
- Every cluster page links to pillar
- Use consistent anchor text
- Link in introduction
- Link in conclusion
- Link in sidebar/navigation

**3. Cluster to Cluster Linking**
- Link related cluster pages
- Use contextual links
- Link when relevant
- Avoid over-linking
- Maintain relevance

**4. Hierarchical Linking**
- Follow content hierarchy
- Link from general to specific
- Link from specific to general
- Create logical flow
- Support topic structure

### Internal Linking Best Practices

**Anchor Text:**
- Use descriptive text
- Include keywords naturally
- Avoid exact match overuse
- Vary anchor text
- Make it relevant

**Link Placement:**
- Link early in content
- Link contextually
- Link in body content
- Use sidebar/navigation
- Use footer links

**Link Quantity:**
- 3-5 internal links per 500 words
- Link to most important pages
- Don't over-link
- Prioritize relevance
- Consider user experience

**Link Maintenance:**
- Fix broken links
- Update old links
- Remove irrelevant links
- Regular audits
- Monitor performance

## Topical Authority vs Domain Authority

### Domain Authority
- Overall site authority
- Based on all backlinks
- Site-wide metric
- General trust signal
- Slow to build

### Topical Authority
- Topic-specific authority
- Based on topic relevance
- Topic-specific metric
- Subject expertise signal
- Can be built faster

### Integration

**Best Approach:**
- Build both simultaneously
- Use topical authority to build domain authority
- Leverage domain authority for new topics
- Focus on topical authority first
- Domain authority follows
